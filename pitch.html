The problem

Independent hotels have great reviews and terrible websites

💸

15-20% to OTAs on every booking

Booking.com and Expedia take a cut on guests who already found the hotel. Without a good direct channel, there's no alternative.

🗺️

Best content is trapped on Google

Great photos, high ratings, real guest trust — but the hotel can't control how any of it looks. It sells Google, not them.

🚫

Nobody designs pages for 9-room guesthouses

A proper website costs thousands and takes weeks. The economics don't work for small properties. So they have a bad one or none.

The insight

The persuasive content already exists

Every hotel already has the material that would sell it — reviews, photos, ratings, location. It's just trapped in a format that sells Google, not the hotel.

The gap is the presentation layer. A machine can now produce it at the cost of one API call.

The differentiator

The design is derived from what guests actually wrote

A hotel whose reviewers keep saying "the silence" gets whitespace and a serif. A hotel where guests time the walk to the station gets a spec sheet.

This isn't a template. It's a design system that responds to what makes each hotel distinct.

AI Innovation

AI is load-bearing, not decorative

🧠

Design Engine

A single LLM call reads reviews, selects one of 7 layout archetypes, and generates a complete design token set — palette, typography, copy, praise themes.

  • ✓ Bans palettes and fonts that look "AI-generated"
  • ✓ Output is JSON, never HTML — structured and editable
  • ✓ Themes paraphrase reviews, never quote directly
🔒

Site Reader

Extraction-only LLM pass over the hotel's existing homepage. Pulls logo, brand palette, typefaces, and booking link.

  • ✓ Not an agent — no tools, no actions, no loop
  • ✓ Page content is untrusted input; zero attack surface
  • ✓ Detects booking engine vendor for auto-tier routing
Business model

The booking button is the pricing axis

Go-to-market

The page is the pitch

We don't ask hotels to sign up and fill in a form. We build the page first and send it to them. The cold email isn't "we made you a page" — it's:

"You're paying Booking.com 18% on every reservation. Here's a page we already built for your property — with your booking button instead of theirs."

The outreach loop is the entire go-to-market. Unapproved pages are sales collateral with a shelf life, not inventory.
